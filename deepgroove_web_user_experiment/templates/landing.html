DeepDrummer Experiment

Step 1/1: Welcome!

Welcome to the DeepDrummer experiment. The purpose of this experiment is to assess how effectively we can train a deep neural network, using limited data, to understand human musical preferences.

The experiment will take place in two phases with a brief pause in between. During each phase, you will be asked to rate a number of short, automatically generated drum loops by clicking the "like" or "dislike" buttons.

During the first phase, the ratings you provide will serve to train the system, and it will try to adjust what it is generating based on the preferences you express. Clicking the "like" button means you would like to guide the system towards generating more like the pattern you have just heard. Clicking "dislike" means you would like to guide the system away from the drum beat you have just heard. If a drum loop sounds unpleasant, annoying or has no musical quality in your view, then you should click "dislike".

Each drum beat will be a short two-second loop repeated 4 times, for a total of 8 seconds. We repeat the pattern 4 times because we find that repetition helps assess musicality. However, you do not have to listen to the full 8 seconds before rating a drum beat. You can feel free to rate each drum beat as soon as you feel confident in your judgment.

The experiment will take approximately 20 minutes of your time and should be completed in one go. We greatly appreciate you taking the time to participate in this short study.
