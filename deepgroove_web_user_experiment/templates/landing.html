DeepDrummer Experiment

Step 1/1: Welcome!

Welcome to the DeepDrummer experiment. The purpose of this experiment is to assess how effectively we can train a deep neural network, using limited data, to understand human musical preferences.

The experiment will take place in two phases with a brief pause in between. During each phase, you will be asked to rate a number of short, automatically generated drum loops as either "good" or "bad".

When we say "good" or "bad", we are asking you to judge whether the drum loop sounds musical or not. We are not asking you to make a judgment as to how original each drum beat sounds. If you could conceive of a drum beat being used in a real musical piece, you should rate it as "good". If the drum loop sounds unpleasant, annoying or has no musical quality in your view, then you should rate it as "bad".

Each drum beat will be a short two-second loop repeated 4 times, for a total of 8 seconds. This is because we find that repetition helps assess musicality. However, you do not have to listen to the full 8 seconds before rating a drum beat as good or bad. You can feel free to rate each drum beat as soon as you feel confident in your judgment.

The experiment will take approximately 20 minutes of your time and should be completed in one go. We greatly appreciate you taking the time to participate in this short study.
